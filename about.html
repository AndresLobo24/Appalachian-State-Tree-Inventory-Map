About This Map

This interactive map represents a detailed inventory of trees located across the Appalachian State University campus in Boone, NC. It is a modern recreation inspired by the original map created by Edward Hyle and his collaborators, available at https://www.appstate.edu/~madritchmd/ASUtree.html.

What is a Cultivar?

A cultivar (short for "cultivated variety") refers to a plant variety that has been selectively bred or cultivated to exhibit specific traits. These traits might include unique leaf color, growth habit, size, flower characteristics, or resistance to pests. Cultivars are usually propagated to maintain these traits consistently.

Example: Acer palmatum 'Bloodgood' is a Japanese maple cultivar prized for its deep purple foliage.

Tree Classifications Explained

  • Deciduous: Trees that shed all their leaves once a year, typically in autumn. Examples: Maples, Oaks, Birches.
  • Evergreen: Trees that retain leaves (or needles) year-round. Examples: Pines, Hollies, Arborvitae.
  • Deciduous Conifer: Cone-bearing trees that lose needles annually. Examples: Bald Cypress, Dawn Redwood, Larch.
  • Evergreen or Deciduous: A flexible category where trees can behave as evergreen or deciduous depending on region. Examples: Some Southern Magnolia or Holly cultivars.
  • Deciduous to Semi-evergreen: Mostly deciduous but may retain some leaves depending on climate or genetics.
  • Semi-evergreen: Retain part of foliage year-round, losing some seasonally. Example: Sweetbay Magnolia in warmer regions.
  • Evergreen Conifer: Cone-bearing trees that remain evergreen. Examples: Pine, Spruce, Fir, Cedar.

About This Project

This interactive map was developed to digitally preserve and modernize the original App State Tree Inventory. It aims to provide enhanced access for students, faculty, and visitors while honoring the work of the original contributors.

Users can explore tree data by scientific name, common name, family, classification, cultivar status, native region, and more. Clicking tree icons opens a popup with details; side-panel filters allow narrowing results interactively.
